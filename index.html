You are a Venturer

You lead with new ideas and embody innovation-readiness.

The first to adopt new ideas or technologies, you enjoy inventing new tools. Change is an exciting event for you. Because you see superior technology and innovation as the path to broad economic change, you feel the need to express yourself by creating ideas and tools that are new and exceptional.

At work you seek to bring visions to life and are often described as an inventor. You’re an intellectual, naturally curious, creative and enterprising. You’re the one to invite and introduce new ideas to fellow travelers from a wide range of sources, and you excel at identifying, refining or designing new products, services or technologies to move everyone forward on the innovation journey.

Venturers travel for the emotional charge of doing it. A daredevil and risk-taker, you explore for the sake of exploring, and try new things simply because they are new. A young-at-heart, adrenaline junkie who is motivated by adventure and discovering new places, you will venture far and wide to find the world’s tallest mountains. You embrace pretty much anything extreme or previously unheard of

VOYAGERS

  • Lead with influence.
  • Are early-adopters and opinion leaders, respected for support of new ideas.
  • Excel in roles that require them to serve as beta testers, first consumers or promoters of new products or services.
  • Question the rules, encourage, facilitate or empower radical change.

DAYTRIPPERS

  • Lead with trusted knowledge.
  • Are early majority adopters of trusted new ideas, and are inspired by others.
  • Are motivated by usefulness and impact.
  • Are team players and could serve as product/service testers.
  • Are concerned about productivity and the practical benefits of new ideas.

TOURISTS

  • Lead with a proven system.
  • Are late majority adopters of new ideas, adopting only after most others have.
  • Are cautious and need to be persuaded about the utility of a new idea before adopting.
  • Prefer to focus on their role in the organization, rather than on new ideas.
  • See change as a sense of belonging.

COMMUTERS

  • Lead within existing systems.
  • Are late majority adopters who are suspicious of change or see it as a threat, from a deep belief in existing systems.
  • Will adopt when forced to, gradually becoming a believer in its value.
  • Are concerned with reliability and cost
  • Are the last to adopt. a new idea, process or technology.
